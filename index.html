Gogo Code

Gogo Code is a program to teach teenage girls in Georgia the basics of computer programming and encourage participation in STEM fields.

What is Gogo Code?

Gogo Code is a program to teach teenage girls in Georgia the basics of computer programming and encourage participation in STEM fields.

Gogo Code is a ten-day coding camp for girls in the Republic of Georgia. It is FREE and open to all girls ages 14-16.

During Camp

Participants will learn:

  • Introductory programming concepts as well as HTML and CSS
  • Hands-on science activities
  • Self-esteem, teamwork, presentation, problem-solving, and critical thinking skills
  • How to make video diaries

After Camp

Girls will participate in:

  • Site visits
  • Community projects
  • Meet-ups
  • Mentoring with professional women

Why coding and STEM?

In Georgia, women and girls are being left on the sidelines in STEM fields (Science, Technology, Engineering and Math).

While girls score slightly higher than boys on Georgian exit exams in STEM subjects, they receive less support, are consistently less confident, and ultimately make up only 16% of university students in IT.

Computer coding is important in every aspect of life, including medicine, community development, and economics.

“If women aren’t represented in technology, their ideas, concerns, and designs won’t be included when we create the cities, cars, infrastructure, medicines, communications, companies, and governments of tomorrow.” - Google’s Made With Code
